Like many LIC residents, you routinely make your way to Greenpoint over the Pulaski bridge, overlooking the notoriously polluted New Town Creek, scraps of industrial waste alongside newly erected luxury condo buildings, leaves ruffling yellowgreen under the morning sun, dimmed ever so slightly by the forthcoming showers.

It's your trustworthy friend. In her solemn black, holding a compact umbrella over her bare head, freshly recovered from the seasonal flu, slowly making her way across the Manhattan Ave, as you gestured the restaurant was in fact on her side... oh well. How have you been? Chitchats on recent reading materials, trip to Savannah which was a real let-down, anticipations or lack thereof of the upcoming season, as your order of borscht arrives in the middle of the table.

None of that unrecognizable tomato-y beef stew back home (which means Harbin for you), the purple liquid is of course made from beet roots, topped with a healthy serving of sour cream. You make a swirl with your spoon and watch the bowl shift color.

What's this now? Kielbasa grilled to a crackling crisp served with a side of gherkins and sauerkraut. Ha! That does strike a chord, garlicky, smoky chunks of meat, bursting bits of rendered fat and a sip of that sweet, tangy borscht like the first time you tasted it from homecooking. Grandfather from Qingdao, grumpy old man, reads exclusively French literature, adores Dumas, in character for a Russian exile, demands regular shippings of such pickle jars and smoked sausages from Harbin. Can't you get it in Qingdao? You see it was a different time back then, transport of goods are not quite where they are today, a peculiar faith was placed in getting that particular thing from that particular place. Local delicacies are local to your neighborhood, district, or at most your city. To go beyond that local network, you need to have connections, so-and-so living there that knows you well, and willing to go through the nuisance of bubblewrapping these glass jars of briny veggies. His daughter was his connection to a taste of that bygone era, or at least the closest approximation of one.

You shouldn't claim to know the guy tho, your grandfather. He did pass away before your frontal lobe was fully developed, not a tear was shed at the news of his passing, and you did not attend his funeral. These bits and pieces merely came back to you on this rainy Sunday afternoon in Greenpoint, by way of palette, the concept of a Grandfather.

And back to sun. You took a stroll in McCarren Park, silent discos going off in the distance, vintage markets, dog walkers and dogs, lots of them. You sat down at a bench, gifts were exchanged. A praline box, and "Wise Blood" by O'Connor, purchased at her childhood house. You listened to her rambling on about how it's impossible to verify a decaf's truly decaf as she sips her cup, and nodded in acquiescence.
