Conveniently located in the culinary vaccuum that is Midtown South (unless if your go-to diet is a combination of corporate slop bowls and dry-aged steaks, in which case, salute to your digestive system), Pizzarte has effectively communicated its ambition for mediocrity through its abomination of a name. But I've seen worse. "The Italian Restaurant" is a fierce competitor on the UWS. Who are the customers of this hole-in-the-wall, semi-basement establishment that could have been converted to a luxary studio apartment? I'd imagine stranded tourists, concert goers grabbing a post-show bite.
